1. CRITICAL ERROR - REQUIRES IMMEDIATE CORRECTION

Week 2, Q7: Incorrect Nutrient Energy Ratio

Question: "Fats provide more energy than carbohydrates. How much more?"

Current Answer: C "Three times as much"

DISTRACTORS (All Options):
  A. Same amount
  B. Twice as much
  C. Three times as much <-- MARKED (INCORRECT)
  D. Half as much

ISSUE: The marked answer C is factually incorrect.

Scientific Fact:
  - Fats provide 9 kilocalories per gram
  - Carbohydrates provide 4 kilocalories per gram
  - Ratio: 9 ÷ 4 = 2.25 times (not 3 times)

Correct Answer Should Be: B "Twice as much" (closest), or ideally a revised
option stating "Approximately 2.25 times as much" or "More than twice"

Reason: This is a factually incorrect statement in the answer key. The energy
density ratio is established biochemical fact. For Grade 6 students learning
nutrition science, accuracy is essential.

Recommendation:
  Option 1 (Preferred): Revise option C to read "Approximately 2.25 times as much"
  Option 2: Change marked answer to B "Twice as much" (conservative estimate)
  Option 3: Add a new correct option and adjust answer key

2. AMBIGUOUS QUESTION - COULD HAVE MULTIPLE CORRECT ANSWERS

Week 1, Q3: Ambiguous Source Classification

Question: "Which of these is obtained from plants?"

Current Answer: D "Wheat"

OPTIONS (All):
  A. Mutton (clearly animal product - incorrect)
  B. Fish (clearly animal product - incorrect)
  C. Honey (bee product, but made from flower nectar - ARGUABLE)
  D. Wheat (plant product - CORRECT as marked)

ISSUE: While wheat (answer D) is the intended correct answer, option C (Honey)
can be scientifically defended as also correct because:
  - Honey is made from flower nectar (which comes from plants)
  - The primary source material of honey is plant material
  - Only the processing organism (bee) is an animal

The question's phrasing "obtained from plants" is ambiguous:
  - If it means "sourced entirely from plants" → D is correct
  - If it means "made from plant material" → C could also be correct

Impact: Low - For Grade 6 level, answer D is the intended and most direct correct
answer. However, a student who selects C could justify their choice scientifically.

Recommendation:
  Option 1 (Preferred): Rephrase question to "Which of these is produced by plants?"
  (to exclude honey unambiguously)
  Option 2: Add clarification to question: "Which of these comes entirely from plants?"
  Option 3: Accept and monitor - most Grade 6 students will select D

3. MINOR SCIENTIFIC IMPRECISION - ACCEPTABLE FOR GRADE 6

Week 2, Q4: Incomplete Description of Bone Composition

Question: "What is the main component of bones?"

Current Answer: B "Calcium"

OPTIONS:
  A. Protein
  B. Calcium <-- MARKED (SIMPLIFIED BUT ACCEPTABLE)
  C. Vitamin A
  D. Carbohydrates

SCIENTIFIC NOTE: Bones are actually composed of:
  - Calcium phosphate and other minerals: ~65%
  - Collagen (protein matrix): ~35%
  - Water: ~10%

While calcium is the PRIMARY MINERAL component and is essential for bone strength,
the organic protein matrix (collagen) is equally important structurally.

Assessment: This is a simplification appropriate for Grade 6 CBSE/ICSE curriculum.
Students at this level are expected to understand that calcium is crucial for
bones, even if not yet studying the protein matrix in detail.

Recommendation: ACCEPT AS IS - This level of simplification is pedagogically
appropriate for 10-12 year olds and aligns with standard Grade 6 curriculum.

---

4. MINOR SCIENTIFIC IMPRECISION - ACCEPTABLE FOR GRADE 6

Week 9, Q14: Misleading Phrasing on Cactus Adaptations

Question: "Spines in cacti are an adaptation for:"

Current Answer: C "Protection and water storage"

OPTIONS:
  A. Photosynthesis
  B. Water conservation
  C. Protection and water storage <-- MARKED
  D. Reproduction

SCIENTIFIC NOTE: Cactus spines function primarily for:
  1. Protection from herbivores and predators (PRIMARY)
  2. Reduction of water loss through reduced leaf surface area (SECONDARY)

The phrase "water storage" is misleading because:
  - Spines do NOT store water
  - They reduce the plant's surface area, thereby reducing water loss
  - The actual water storage in cacti occurs in the fleshy stems/tissues, not the spines
  - A more accurate description: "Protection and reduced water loss"

Option B says "Water conservation" which is actually MORE accurate than the marked
answer's "water storage."

Assessment: This is a minor phrasing issue. The intended concept is correct (spines
reduce water loss), but the terminology is imprecise.

Recommendation: IMPROVE (Optional) - Change option C to read
"Protection and water conservation" to be more scientifically precise.

Alternative: Accept as is, since Grade 6 students understand the general concept
that spines help cacti survive in deserts.
